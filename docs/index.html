01 · Media

When the media got louder

How often Mbappé appeared in GDELT-tracked news, and what the surrounding article tone looked like. Media coverage in this project comes from GDELT, a public dataset that monitors global news sources across web, print and broadcast media.

Coverage means GDELT-tracked articles that mention Mbappé. It does not mean every article published online and does not always mean the article is mainly about him. Still, because GDELT monitors a large set of news sources, the coverage is useful as a broad signal of how media attention changes over time. To separate passing mentions from stronger focus, this page also shows a high-salience subset: articles where Mbappé is more likely to be a main topic. The estimate is based on metadata and entity context extracted by GDELT for each article.

Notes:

  • Because GDELT context was limited for non-English translated records, Mbappé was identified mainly through URL mentions in the French and Spanish series. These series are useful as broad URL-based mention signals, but they are not full high-salience equivalents. They may undercount weeks when Mbappé was discussed without appearing in the article URL.
  • A GDELT service incident left source data unavailable from 15 June through 1 July 2025, creating a visible gap in the media series.

02 · Public attention

Where curiosity rose

Media coverage and public curiosity are related, but they are not the same thing. News can rise without people searching more, and public attention can rise because of matches, viral clips, transfers or social media.

03 · Performance

What was happening on the pitch

Match performance provides football context for the media timeline. Goals, minutes, opponents and competitions can help explain why a week matters, but they do not prove what caused media coverage or public attention to rise.
